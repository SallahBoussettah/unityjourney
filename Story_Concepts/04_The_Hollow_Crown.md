# THE HOLLOW CROWN
## Medieval Political RPG / Intrigue | 45-60 min Demo

---

## ELEVATOR PITCH
A mercenary arrives in a medieval capital on the eve of civil war. The king is poisoned, the prince is missing, and five noble houses are circling the throne. But the real threat isn't politics — it's the ancient civilization awakening beneath the city's foundations. A third-person RPG where your WORDS are as dangerous as your sword.

---

## STORY PREMISE

The Kingdom of Aldenmere has stood for three hundred years, built on the ruins of something far older. The capital city — Kingshold — sits atop a network of underground passages, ancient dwarven construction that predates human settlement by millennia. The founders of Aldenmere called it a foundation. They were wrong. It was a ceiling.

King Aldric III was found in his chambers three days ago, unconscious, veins blackened by an unidentifiable poison. He breathes, but does not wake. Crown Prince Edric vanished the same night — some say he fled in guilt, others say he was taken. The five noble houses of Kingshold have suspended their feuds just long enough to assign blame to each other:

- **House Valorian** (the clergy) controls the Gothic Cathedral and claims divine authority to choose the next king
- **House Merrow** (the merchants) controls the docks and trade, threatening to starve the city if denied influence
- **House Ashfall** (the military) holds the city garrison and wants martial law
- **House Voss** (the scholars) occupies the Library and claims to know who poisoned the king — for a price
- **House Greymoor** (the old blood) holds the ancient charter that grants succession rights, but their heir is a child

Into this arrives you. A mercenary. No family name, no allegiance, no stake in the game. You received a letter — unsigned, sealed with black wax — offering a fortune in gold for "one night's work in Kingshold." The letter contained a single instruction: "Come to the Bakery on Threadneedle Lane at dusk. Tell no one."

The demo covers one night. From dusk to dawn, the player navigates Kingshold's political labyrinth, gathering information, making alliances, and uncovering a conspiracy that dwarfs the succession crisis. Because the poisoning wasn't about politics. It was about what lies beneath the city. And it's waking up.

---

## MAIN THEMES

- **Power and corruption** — Every faction has a noble reason and a selfish one. The player decides which to believe.
- **Loyalty for sale** — You're a mercenary. Everything is transactional. Or is it?
- **The sins of colonization** — Aldenmere was built on stolen land. The original inhabitants were driven underground. Now they're returning.
- **Surface vs. depth** — Literally (city above, ancient city below) and metaphorically (public faces vs. hidden agendas)
- **The weight of a single night** — Every choice in a compressed timeframe has amplified consequences

## NARRATIVE STRUCTURE

### ACT 1 — "THE CAPITAL" (~15 min)

**Arrival at Dusk**
The player enters Kingshold through the main gate. The Modular Medieval Town with Interior stretches before them — cobblestone streets, timber-frame buildings with full interiors, market stalls being shuttered for the night, guards patrolling in pairs. The town is tense. Citizens hurry home. Shutters close as you pass.

**The Bakery Meeting**
Following the letter's instructions, the player reaches the Modular Bakery Shop on Threadneedle Lane. Inside, a hooded figure waits (using Adventure Characters Pack). This is your contact — a spy for House Voss who has been investigating the poisoning independently. They give you two things:
1. A dossier on the five houses (delivered as Inventory items — readable documents)
2. A warning: "Don't trust anyone who tells you they know what happened. Everyone is lying. Including me."

The contact is poisoned mid-conversation. The same black-veined poison that struck the king. They die in your arms. Their last words: "The carpenter... knows..."

**Free Exploration of Kingshold**
The player is now free to explore the capital. This is a semi-open hub with multiple locations accessible in any order:

- **The Carpenter's Workshop:** A craftsman (NPC from Fantasy Characters Pack) who made a special order three days ago — a lockbox with dwarven symbols. He doesn't know what it was for, but he knows who ordered it: a member of House Greymoor. He's terrified and will only talk if you promise protection (Dialogue choice).

- **The 1850s Post Office (Town Message Board):** Public notices, bounty boards, and — hidden among them — coded messages between house agents. The player can collect these as evidence.

- **The Pawn Shop:** Run by a shrewd dealer (Pawn Shop System). She doesn't just sell — she trades in INFORMATION. Sell her an item from a noble house, and she'll tell you what she knows about them. The economy IS intelligence gathering.

- **The Fantasy Interior Library (House Voss Territory):** Scholars guard access jealously. With the right approach (Dialogue System), the player can access historical records about the dwarven civilization below Kingshold. A crucial document reveals: the kingdom's founding charter includes a clause written in a language no one reads anymore. A scholar translates it: "We borrow this land from those below. When the Compact ends, so does the city."

- **The Lookout Tower:** Climb to the top for a view of the entire city. From here, you can see torchlight moving in the cemetery (House Greymoor is doing something at the graves) and smoke rising from the docks (House Merrow is burning documents).

**Invitations**
Before Act 1 ends, the player receives two invitations delivered by house servants:
- House Valorian invites you to the Gothic Cathedral for a "prayer service" (actually a political meeting)
- House Merrow invites you to the Laketown docks for a "business proposition"

The player MUST choose one. They cannot attend both. This is the first major branching point.

---

### ACT 2 — "THE LONG NIGHT" (~25 min)

The story branches based on the player's choice. Both paths reveal different pieces of the conspiracy and converge in Act 3.

**PATH A — THE CATHEDRAL (House Valorian)**

The UCreate Gothic Cathedral is stunning at night — candlelit, shadows dancing on stone walls, stained glass dark from outside but glowing from within. The High Priestess of Valorian (Fantasy Characters Pack) meets the player in the nave.

Her pitch: "The king was poisoned by House Merrow to destabilize the kingdom. They want to install a puppet ruler who will open the borders to foreign trade — flooding Aldenmere with outside influence and destroying our traditions. We need a skilled blade to... resolve this."

She's offering you gold to assassinate House Merrow's leader.

**Dialogue options:**
- Accept the job (gains Valorian trust, enemy of Merrow)
- Refuse but stay friendly (neutral, gains some information)
- Challenge her evidence (she reveals more about the dwarven connection — Valorian has been performing rituals to "appease the old ones" for centuries, and they've recently stopped working)
- Accuse Valorian of the poisoning (she's furious but lets something slip — she knew about the dwarven lockbox before you mentioned it)

After the meeting, the player exits the cathedral to find the Ithris Cemetery lit with torches. House Greymoor is performing an exhumation. Investigation reveals: they're digging up the founder's grave. Inside the coffin, no body — just the lockbox the carpenter made. Inside the lockbox: a key made of material that isn't metal, isn't stone, isn't anything human. It's dwarven.

**Combat encounter:** The Assassin character ambushes you in the cemetery. Fast, deadly, using poison-coated blades. This is a genuine threat — the Parry Attack System is essential. After defeating (or driving off) the assassin, you find a Greymoor sigil on their belt. But is it genuine, or planted?

**PATH B — THE DOCKS (House Merrow)**

Laketown at night. Warehouses, fishing boats, the smell of salt and tar. House Merrow's leader — a heavyset merchant (G2: Mercenaries character reskinned) — meets you on his flagship.

His pitch: "House Valorian poisoned the king to install a theocracy. They've been performing dark rituals in the cathedral basement for years. We need someone who can break into the cathedral and find proof."

He's offering you gold to infiltrate the cathedral and steal evidence.

**Dialogue options:**
- Accept the job (gains Merrow trust, enemy of Valorian)
- Refuse but stay friendly (neutral, gains some information)
- Challenge his evidence (he reveals more — Merrow shipping records show they've been importing dwarven artifacts from abroad. Someone is buying the old civilization's belongings. Why?)
- Accuse Merrow of the poisoning (he laughs. "If I wanted the king dead, he'd be dead, not sleeping.")

After the meeting, the player discovers Merrow's warehouses (using Construction Site props for a dockside industrial feel) contain more than trade goods. A hidden basement connects to the Cave Ruins — an underground passage leading toward the Dwarven City. Merrow has been excavating secretly.

**Combat encounter:** Skeleton Guards emerge from the passage. They're not hostile at first — they march in formation, as if patrolling. But when they see the player, they attack. These are the awakened dead of the original dwarven civilization. They're not undead in the traditional sense — they're soldiers responding to an ancient alarm.

---

### ACT 3 — "THE TREMOR" (~15 min)

Both paths converge. The player now has partial evidence from their chosen house AND their own investigation. They know:
- The dwarven civilization beneath Kingshold is awakening
- Multiple houses have been interacting with dwarven artifacts/ruins
- The poisoning is connected to an ancient "Compact" between the founders and the dwarves
- Someone triggered this deliberately

**The Underground**
Following the cave passage (from Path B) or a cathedral crypt entrance (from Path A), the player descends into the Dwarven City Modular Environment. It is massive, ancient, and lit by bioluminescent crystals (Stylized Modular Crystal Mine assets for crystal growths). The architecture is alien — too precise, too geometric, inhuman in its perfection.

Here the player encounters:
- **Skeleton Knights** — elite dwarven soldiers, harder enemies
- **Lizardman Berserker** — a guardian species that served the dwarves, now awakened
- **Environmental puzzles** using Gathering Resources (clear rubble) and Ultimate Interaction Manager (activate ancient mechanisms)

In the deepest chamber, the player finds the truth: a massive stone tablet inscribed with the Compact. It reads:

*"To the surface-dwellers who found our ceiling: we grant you three hundred years above. Build your homes, raise your children, live your lives. When the three hundred years end, we reclaim what is ours. This is not a threat. This is a lease."*

Today is the 300th anniversary of Aldenmere's founding.

The king wasn't poisoned by any house. He was poisoned by the Compact itself — a magical clause that removes the ruler when the lease expires. The prince wasn't kidnapped — he was summoned below by the dwarves for "renegotiation."

**The Climax**
The player returns to the surface. The town square. Every house has gathered — chaos, accusations, drawn swords. The player can:

1. **Reveal the truth to all houses** (Dialogue System — requires evidence from investigation). United, they prepare for what's coming. But unity is fragile.
2. **Reveal the truth to their chosen house only** (gives that house an advantage). The other houses are blindsided.
3. **Keep the truth and leverage it** (maximum personal power, minimum collective preparation).

Regardless of choice, the ground shakes. Cobblestones crack. From beneath the town square, a massive arm of stone and crystal pushes through — the Ancient Golem, the dwarven emissary, emerging to deliver a message.

A Comic Book Cutscene shows the golem's eyes opening. It speaks in a voice like grinding stone:

*"The lease is concluded. The renegotiation begins. Send us your representative. Or we will come for everyone."*

The demo ends with the golem standing in the ruined town square, noble houses in disarray, and the player — a mercenary with no allegiance — suddenly the only person all factions might trust to go below.

Title card: **THE HOLLOW CROWN**

Text: *"Demo Complete. The full game follows the renegotiation — a journey into the Dwarven City where diplomacy, combat, and ancient secrets determine the fate of Aldenmere."*

## THE UNIQUE MECHANIC: THE INVESTIGATION-DIPLOMACY LOOP

**How it works:**
1. **Investigate:** Explore Kingshold, find evidence (documents, artifacts, witness testimony)
2. **Collect:** Evidence goes into Inventory as readable/presentable items
3. **Trade:** At the Pawn Shop, sell items for gold OR for information about factions
4. **Confront:** Present evidence to faction leaders in Dialogue scenes
5. **Choose:** Each revelation opens new dialogue options with other factions
6. **Consequence:** Your evidence presentation changes faction disposition — alliances form and break

**The Pawn Shop as Intelligence Hub:**
- The shopkeeper has connections to every house
- What you SELL tells her what you've been doing (sell a Valorian artifact = she knows you've been in the cathedral)
- What you BUY signals your intentions (buy lockpicks = she knows you plan to break in somewhere)
- She is the most dangerous NPC in the game — not because she fights, but because she TALKS

**Dialogue consequences:**
- Accuse a faction of the poisoning → they become hostile, but their enemies become friendly
- Share evidence freely → factions trust you more, but you lose leverage
- Withhold evidence → maintain power, but factions distrust you
- Lie about evidence → short-term advantage, but if caught, ALL factions turn hostile

---

## THE UNIQUE MECHANIC: BRANCHING NIGHT

**How it works:**
1. The demo takes place over ONE NIGHT — compressed real-time
2. Choosing Path A (Cathedral) vs Path B (Docks) changes Act 2 entirely
3. Both paths reveal DIFFERENT pieces of the conspiracy — no single playthrough gives the full picture
4. This creates organic replayability: players WANT to replay to see the other path
5. Act 3 converges but the player's knowledge (and faction relationships) differ based on their path

**Why branching works in a demo:**
- The demo is short (45-60 min) — replaying isn't a huge investment
- Players who replay feel they got MORE value from the demo
- The different paths generate discussion ("Did you go to the Cathedral or the Docks?")
- This discussion drives word-of-mouth marketing

---

## WHY THIS WORKS FOR A SOLO DEV

1. **Modular Medieval Town with Interior is the single most valuable asset** — it provides a complete hub with building interiors, eliminating the need for custom architecture
2. **One hub, two paths** — branching content doubles perceived size with less than double the work (Act 1 and Act 3 are shared)
3. **Dialogue-heavy gameplay requires writing, not art** — the cheapest content to produce for the highest narrative impact
4. **The "one night" framing limits scope naturally** — no need for a day/night cycle, weather system, or NPC schedules beyond a single night
5. **The Pawn Shop System provides economy out of the box** — just needs inventory items to populate it
6. **Moore's RPG Template handles quest management** — reducing custom systems needed
7. **Combat is reserved for high-stakes moments** — less combat = less animation/balance work
8. **The Dwarven City as Act 3 payoff is visually stunning** — players remember how the demo ENDS, and this ending is spectacular
9. **Political intrigue is underserved in the indie space** — most medieval games focus on combat. This stands out.
