# ASHEN LITANY
## Dark Fantasy Soulslike | 30-45 min Demo

---

## ELEVATOR PITCH
In a world where prayers have physical form, you play a mute bell-ringer in an abandoned cathedral who discovers that the last prayer ever spoken is trapped inside the bells — and something is eating it from the inside. A third-person atmospheric action-exploration game where combat IS prayer.

---

## STORY PREMISE

The world ended not with a scream, but with a silence. The gods stopped listening. When they did, the prayers of the faithful — once invisible whispers carried skyward — solidified into raw energy with nowhere to go. This energy, called Litany, pooled in sacred places: cathedrals, crypts, graveyards. It seeped into the dead and woke them. It fused with stone and birthed golems. It corrupted the faithful and twisted them into hollow guardians of places no god would ever visit again.

You are the Bell-Ringer. You have no name. You have no memory. You awaken on the cold stone floor of a cathedral nave, your hands wrapped in bandages stained with old blood, a iron bell-hammer at your side. The cathedral is vast, crumbling, and infested with the reanimated bones of those who prayed here last. Stained glass windows depict a story you cannot remember but feel in your chest like a wound.

As you explore the cathedral, its cemetery grounds, and the crypts beneath, you discover fragments of Litany — crystallized prayers that whisper when you touch them. Each fragment reveals a piece of what happened: the last congregation gathered here when the silence began. They prayed harder. They prayed until their voices broke. The Litany they produced was so powerful it cracked the foundation of the cathedral and opened a passage to something ancient below — a place called The Sanctum, where an artifact known as The Orb holds the accumulated faith of ten thousand years.

The bell towers still stand. And when you ring a bell, the Litany resonates — and parts of the cathedral that were consumed by darkness become visible again. Entire wings, hidden chambers, sealed crypts — they exist only while the bell's tone holds. The cathedral is far larger than it appears. But something lurks in the resonance. Each bell you ring draws the attention of the Hollow Choir — corrupted bell-ringers who came before you, consumed by the Litany they tried to wield.

The demo ends with a revelation: you find your own name carved into the wall of the deepest crypt, alongside a date. You have been here before. You were the FIRST bell-ringer. And the reason you have no memory is because you gave it willingly — as the price for one final prayer.

## MAIN THEMES

- **Faith without gods** — What does devotion mean when no one is listening?
- **Sacrifice and forgetting** — The protagonist gave everything, including their identity
- **The weight of silence** — A world defined by the absence of something sacred
- **Corruption of the pure** — Prayers, the most innocent human act, became the source of horror
- **Cycles and repetition** — You've done this before. The question is whether this time will be different.

## NARRATIVE STRUCTURE

### ACT 1 — "THE NAVE" (~10 min)

You awaken face-down on cracked stone tiles. Dust motes drift through shafts of pale light from broken stained glass above. The cathedral nave stretches before you — vast, silent, beautiful in its ruin.

**Tutorial gameplay:** Learn movement, light attack, heavy attack, dodge. Your weapon is an iron bell-hammer — heavy, deliberate, resonant. Each swing produces a faint chime. When you strike enemies, the chime grows louder.

**First encounters:** Skeleton Guards patrol the nave in slow, processional patterns — as if still performing a religious ceremony. They are not aggressive until disturbed. This teaches the player: you can observe before engaging, and these were once people performing sacred duties.

**Environmental storytelling:** Readable prayer fragments on the walls (delivered via Dialogue System for Lore Building). They start hopeful ("The gods will hear us") and grow desperate ("Why won't they answer?"). The progression tells the story without a single cutscene.

**Act 1 climax:** You reach the bell tower base. A massive iron bell hangs above. When you ring it, the sound wave ripples outward — and an entire sealed wing of the cathedral materializes from darkness. The walls glow with Litany. A passage opens that wasn't there before.

**Emotional beat:** Wonder. The world is bigger than you thought. What else is hidden?

---

### ACT 2 — "THE GROUNDS" (~15 min)

The bell's resonance revealed the passage to the cemetery grounds outside. You step into grey daylight under Chaotic Skies — the sky is fractured, bruised purple and burning orange, as if reality itself is wounded. The Ithris Cemetery sprawls across rolling hills. Tombstones, mausoleums, dead trees. And the dead walk here too.

**Combat escalation:** Skeleton Knights are faster, hit harder, and can parry YOUR attacks. You must learn the Parry system to survive. Each successful parry produces a harmonic chime — chain parries into a "Prayer Combo" that releases a burst of Litany energy (healing + area damage). This is the core combat loop: offense IS defense IS healing.

**Exploration:** The cemetery is semi-open. Multiple paths lead through mausoleums (using Gothic Cathedral assets for small chapel interiors), across a ruined watermill bridge (Watermills/Nature Ruin), and past a lookout tower that gives a vista of the shattered world below the cathedral — an endless dark chasm where the earth cracked open.

**Mini-boss: The Wood Monster.** A tree in the center of the cemetery that isn't a tree. It's a massive creature of bark and bone, a fusion of nature and Litany. It attacks with sweeping roots and thrown debris. Defeating it drops a Litany Fragment that reveals: the cemetery holds the bodies of the original congregation. They didn't die of the silence — they were SACRIFICED to fuel the final prayer.

**Discovery:** At the far end of the cemetery, a crypt entrance leads underground. Above the entrance, carved in stone: "Ring the second bell, and the Sanctum opens."

**Emotional beat:** Growing dread. The sacred place has a bloody history.

---

### ACT 3 — "THE CRYPT" (~15 min)

Descent into the Cave Ruins beneath the cemetery. The architecture shifts from religious to ancient — dwarven stonework, crystal veins in the walls (Crystal Mine assets), and deeper still, modular dungeon corridors lined with traps (Assortment of Traps).

**Puzzle section:** Litany crystals block passages. The player must use Element Reactions (fire Litany melts ice barriers, lightning Litany activates ancient mechanisms) to progress. The Element Reaction System provides combat variety AND puzzle solutions.

**Lore deepening:** You find the remains of previous bell-ringers. Each skeleton holds a journal fragment. They all came here. They all rang the bells. They all descended to The Sanctum. None returned. Their final entries all say the same thing: "I remember now."

**The Second Bell Chamber:** Deep in the crypt, a second bell tower — this one inverted, hanging into the chasm below. Ringing it causes the dungeon to SHIFT — walls move, new passages open, and the environment transforms (Nanite Dungeon Pack replacing the cave ruins, showing the true architecture beneath). This is the Sanctum revealing itself.

**Boss: The First of the Hollow Choir.** A corrupted bell-ringer, seven feet tall, wielding a cracked bell on a chain. They fight with rhythmic, musical attacks — each swing produces a discordant tone that damages you if you're within the sound radius. You must learn their rhythm and counter it with your own. Prayer Combos are essential. When defeated, the Hollow Choir member dissolves into Litany and their bell cracks — releasing a memory. YOUR memory. You see yourself, in this same crypt, long ago, ringing this same bell. You were here. You CHOSE to forget.

**Emotional beat:** Horror of recognition. The cycle is real.

---

### DEMO ENDING — "THE REVELATION"

The boss's death opens the final passage. You step into the SciFi Dark Fantasy environment — The Orb's chamber. It is alien, wrong, beautiful. A massive sphere of crystallized Litany floats above a chasm of pure light. Ten thousand years of accumulated faith, compressed into a single object. The air hums.

A Comic Book Cutscene triggers:

*Flashes of memory — you, kneeling before The Orb. A congregation behind you, chanting. You were their champion. You were going to carry their final prayer to the gods. But The Orb showed you the truth: the gods didn't stop listening. They were never there. The prayers were always just... energy. Meaningless. Beautiful. Meaningless.*

*You couldn't tell them. So you rang every bell. You sealed the cathedral. You erased your own memory. And you walked back to the nave to sleep. To forget. To let the silence be enough.*

*But the Litany kept growing. The dead kept waking. And someone — something — rang a bell that woke you up.*

The screen reads: **"The Orb remembers what you chose to forget."**

Cut to black. Title card: **ASHEN LITANY**.

Text: *"Demo Complete. The full game will answer the question: What happens when you ring all the bells?"*

## THE UNIQUE MECHANIC: PRAYER COMBAT

**How it works:**
1. Every melee attack is a "syllable" of prayer
2. Completing a full combo without interruption = completing a prayer
3. Completed prayers trigger effects based on the combo pattern:
   - Light-Light-Heavy = "Prayer of Wrath" (AoE Litany burst)
   - Light-Parry-Heavy = "Prayer of Resilience" (heal + defense buff)
   - Heavy-Heavy-Light = "Prayer of Sight" (reveals hidden objects briefly)
4. The Melee Weapon Aura VFX glows brighter with each successful syllable
5. Getting hit mid-combo "breaks" the prayer — the energy dissipates harmlessly
6. This creates a risk/reward loop: commit to the full prayer for the reward, or play safe with single hits

**How bells work:**
1. Bell towers are found throughout the game
2. Ringing a bell sends a resonance wave through the level
3. While the tone sustains (~30 seconds), hidden geometry becomes visible and traversable
4. Some bells reveal permanently; others require repeated ringing (creating timed puzzle sections)
5. Each bell also serves as a checkpoint / fast travel point
6. The sound design IS the game — the bells, the chimes of combat, the silence between

## WHY THIS WORKS FOR A SOLO DEV

1. **Two cathedral packs + cemetery + dungeon packs = complete, cohesive world** with zero custom 3D art
2. **Indoor/underground focus** eliminates open-world scope problems
3. **Limited character roster:** one protagonist, skeletal enemies (modular = visual variety), one nature boss, one humanoid boss
4. **Bell mechanic** is technically just level streaming + post-process — spectacular visually, simple technically
5. **Prayer combat** is a flavor layer on existing Melee Combo + Parry systems
6. **Environmental storytelling** requires writing, not voice acting or cinematics
7. **Proven market:** dark fantasy soulslikes consistently perform well (Blasphemous sold 1M+, Ender Lilies sold 1.5M+)
8. **Tight scope:** one interconnected location, 30-40 minutes, clear beginning/middle/end
