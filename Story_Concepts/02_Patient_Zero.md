# PATIENT ZERO
## Psychological Horror / Mystery | 30-45 min Demo

---

## ELEVATOR PITCH
A physician receives an anonymous call luring her to an abandoned hospital where she discovers that the pharmaceutical catastrophe she's investigating leads back to her own childhood — she was Patient Zero. A first-person exploration-horror game where VHS tapes on CRT TVs are the primary storytelling device.

---

## STORY PREMISE

Dr. Sarah Chen is a physician haunted by a career built on silence. Five years ago, she worked for Helios Pharmaceuticals — a biotech company that promised miracle cures. She left when she discovered irregularities in their clinical trial data, but she never reported them. She told herself it wasn't her responsibility. She told herself someone else would notice. No one did.

Three weeks ago, 247 people in a coastal town called Greyhaven died in a single night. The official cause: contaminated water supply. But Sarah recognizes the symptoms in the autopsy reports. They match the side effects she saw in the Helios trial data — the data she chose to ignore.

Tonight, Sarah receives an anonymous phone call. A distorted voice tells her that proof of Helios's crimes is hidden inside Greyhaven Memorial Hospital — an abandoned facility on the outskirts of town that was shut down fifteen years ago. The caller says the hospital was where it all began. The first trials. The first deaths. And the evidence is still inside, waiting.

Sarah drives to Greyhaven as the sun sets. The town is a ghost of itself — most residents either dead or evacuated. She parks outside the hospital. The building looms against a stormy sky, dark except for a single flickering light on the third floor. Her phone buzzes: a text from an unknown number. "The front door is unlocked. Start in the lobby. I'll guide you."

What Sarah doesn't know — what she won't discover until the final act — is that she has been to this hospital before. Not as a doctor. As a child. She was the first test subject. Patient Zero. The reason she survived the Helios drug is that it was tested on her first, at age seven, in this very building. Her memories of it were suppressed — chemically erased by the same company that employed her as an adult.

The anonymous caller is another survivor. They remember everything. And they didn't bring Sarah here just for evidence. They brought her here to remember.

---

## MAIN THEMES

- **Complicity and guilt** — What is the cost of looking away?
- **Corporate evil and human cost** — Faceless institutions, real victims
- **Suppressed memory and trauma** — The body remembers what the mind forgets
- **Truth vs. self-preservation** — Is knowing always better than not knowing?
- **The banality of horror** — The scariest things happen in fluorescent-lit rooms with paperwork

## NARRATIVE STRUCTURE

### ACT 1 — "THE CALL" (~10 min)

**Opening:** A Comic Book Cutscene in stark black-and-white shows Sarah at her apartment, staring at news coverage of the Greyhaven disaster. Her phone rings. The caller's voice is distorted but calm: "You know what Helios did. I can prove it. Greyhaven Memorial Hospital. Tonight. Come alone."

**Gameplay begins:** Sarah arrives at Laketown — the coastal approach to Greyhaven. It's dusk. Rain is starting. The town is eerily empty. She drives (or walks) through the main street, passing the 1850s Post Office (repurposed as the town's community notice board — missing persons flyers, quarantine notices, a handwritten sign reading "GOD HELP US"). She can explore the Carpenter's Workshop (door ajar, tools scattered mid-project — the owner left in a hurry) and the Modular Bakery Shop (bread still on the counter, hardened and grey).

**Atmosphere building:** No enemies. No jump scares. Just emptiness. The horror is in the absence of people. Radios play static. A dog's water bowl sits full outside a house. The VHS rental store (using CRT TV assets) has a TV playing snow in the window.

**Arrival at the hospital:** Sarah reaches the Abandoned Hospital. It's massive. Brutalist concrete, shattered windows, overgrown grounds. The front door is, as promised, unlocked. She steps inside. Her phone buzzes: "Lobby. Look for the reception desk. The first file is there."

**She picks up a flashlight from her car.** The Flashlight becomes her primary tool for the rest of the game. Battery is limited (indicated by dimming light). Backup batteries are scattered but scarce. FlashMate serves as an emergency backup with a different (weaker, more blue) light quality.

**Emotional beat:** Isolation. Dread. The sense of walking into something you can't walk out of.

---

### ACT 2 — "WARD BY WARD" (~20 min)

The hospital is the core of the game. It is explored floor by floor, ward by ward. Each section reveals more of the story, escalating from clinical unease to outright horror.

**THE LOBBY / RECEPTION**
- Dusty reception desk, overturned wheelchairs, a dead plant
- Sarah finds the first patient file: "Subject 001 — Age 7 — Female — Trial Group Alpha"
- The file is heavily redacted. Only fragments visible: "...initial dose administered... subject displayed acute distress... memory consolidation disrupted..."
- Phone buzzes: "That file is you, Sarah. Keep going. Third floor. Pediatric wing."

**FIRST FLOOR — GENERAL WARD**
- Long corridors of patient rooms (Assortment of Doors — most locked, some openable)
- Each room tells a micro-story through environmental details: a room with restraint marks on the bed frame, a room where someone scratched tally marks into the wall (147 marks), a room with children's drawings taped to the wall — the drawings are of needles and crying faces
- Industry Props Pack provides medical equipment: IV stands, monitors, surgical trays
- Construction PROPS for deterioration: crumbling walls, debris, water damage
- LED Light Generator for flickering emergency lighting in certain corridors

**THE FIRST VHS TAPE**
- In a break room, Sarah finds an old CRT TV and a VHS tape labeled "ORIENTATION — NEW STAFF"
- She inserts the tape. The Advanced CRT TV / VCR / VHS Effects kick in — scan lines, tracking distortion, washed-out color
- The tape shows a cheerful corporate video from 1997: "Welcome to Greyhaven Memorial Hospital, a Helios Pharmaceuticals research partner!" It tours the facility, showing clean halls and smiling staff. But in the background of one shot, through a window, you can see a child being led down a corridor by two orderlies. The child is struggling.
- The tape cuts to static.

**SECOND FLOOR — ADMINISTRATIVE WING**
- The Conference / Meeting Room Environment is used here: a large boardroom with Helios logos, scattered documents, a shattered projector screen
- Sarah finds meeting minutes from 2009: discussions about "Phase 3 subject mortality rates" and "acceptable loss parameters"
- A locked filing cabinet (Ultimate Interaction Manager puzzle — find the key in the adjacent office) contains a USB drive. Sarah's phone buzzes: "You can't read that here. Take it. It's for after."
- In the administrator's office: a second CRT TV. A VHS tape labeled "SECURITY CAM — PED WING — 03/14/1998"
- The tape shows a dimly lit corridor from a ceiling camera. A small girl in a hospital gown walks alone. She stops, looks directly at the camera, and mouths something. The tracking distorts. When it clears, the corridor is empty.

**Phone interaction:** Throughout Act 2, the Advanced Phone System delivers texts from the anonymous caller. They start helpful ("Turn left at the stairwell") and become more personal ("Do you remember the smell of antiseptic? You used to cry when you smelled it. You still do, don't you?"). The caller knows details about Sarah that Sarah herself doesn't understand.

**THIRD FLOOR — PEDIATRIC WING (The Horror Peaks)**
- This is the most disturbing section. Small beds. Cartoon murals peeling off walls. A playroom with scattered toys — but the toys are medical equipment (toy stethoscopes, a toy syringe).
- Sarah finds a room with "SARAH" scratched into the wall by a child's hand. Below it: a crude drawing of a woman in a white coat holding a needle, and a small figure crying.
- **The Line of Sight Dynamic Mesh system activates here:** Sarah's visibility cone becomes narrow and claustrophobic. Shadows at the edges of her flashlight beam seem to move. The player THINKS they see a figure at the end of a corridor. When they aim the flashlight: nothing. Or was there?
- **Horror encounters:** The Zombie Lite system provides ghostly, shambling figures that appear at the periphery of vision. They never fully materialize. You never fight them. They might be hallucinations — residual chemicals in the hospital's air system? The game never confirms or denies. This ambiguity IS the horror.
- **Third VHS tape:** Found in the head nurse's station. Labeled "SESSION 7 — SUBJECT 001." The tape shows a clinical room. A girl (age ~7) sits in a chair while a doctor administers an injection. The girl screams. The doctor makes notes. The girl goes silent. Her eyes are open but vacant. The doctor speaks to the camera: "Subject 001 has entered the dissociative state. Memory consolidation appears fully disrupted. Proceeding to Phase 2." The doctor's name badge is visible for one frame: "Dr. Marcus Webb." Sarah's former supervisor at Helios.

**BASEMENT — THE EVIDENCE ROOM**
- A passage from the pediatric wing leads down to the basement
- City Subway Train Modular assets create an underground passage — the hospital connected to an underground network (for discreet transport of subjects)
- Mystery Cave assets for a raw, unfinished tunnel section — evidence was being moved to be destroyed, but the operation was abandoned mid-way
- Sarah finds boxes of files, hard drives, and a final VHS tape

**Final VHS tape:** "EXIT INTERVIEW — SUBJECT 001 — AGE 7." A girl sits across from a woman in a suit. The woman says: "Sarah, you're going home today. Your new family is very nice. You won't remember any of this. That's okay. It's better that way." The girl nods. She is holding a stuffed rabbit. The camera zooms to the girl's face. It is unmistakably a young Sarah.

**Emotional beat:** Escalating horror. The transition from "investigating a crime" to "discovering you ARE the crime."

---

### ACT 3 — "PATIENT ZERO" (~10 min)

Sarah stumbles out of the basement into the cemetery behind the hospital. The Ithris Cemetery is where the failed test subjects were buried — unmarked graves, rows and rows of them. The Matte Painting Skybox shows a stormy, apocalyptic sky. The Clouds Lighting System flickers with distant lightning.

She walks through the graves. Her phone buzzes one final time: "I'm here."

A figure steps out from behind a mausoleum. The Survival Character — a man in his 30s, gaunt, scarred. He introduces himself as Daniel. Subject 023. He was seven years old when they tested the drug on him. Unlike Sarah, his memories were never erased. He's lived with them for 25 years.

**Dialogue System scene:** Daniel and Sarah talk. The player can choose Sarah's responses:
- Ask about the evidence ("What do we do with all this?")
- Ask about herself ("What was I like? When I was here?")
- Ask about the deaths ("The 247 people — was it Helios?")
- Express guilt ("I could have stopped this. I had the data.")

Daniel reveals: Helios never stopped. The Greyhaven disaster wasn't contaminated water. It was a new version of the same drug, deployed in the municipal water supply as an unauthorized mass trial. The 247 deaths were "acceptable losses" — the same language from the boardroom minutes.

He hands Sarah a USB drive. "Everything. Every file. Every name. Every child. Including yours."

**The choice (demo ending):**
A Comic Book Cutscene presents the final decision:
- **Take the drive to the media.** Sarah walks toward her car. The screen shows a montage: news broadcasts, courtrooms, Helios executives in handcuffs. But also: Sarah's face on every screen. Her past exposed. She will never be anonymous again.
- **Walk away.** Sarah drops the drive. She gets in her car. She drives. The road stretches ahead. The rearview mirror shows Daniel standing alone in the cemetery, getting smaller. A text appears on her phone: "They'll do it again."

The screen fades to black. Title card: **PATIENT ZERO**.

Text: *"Demo Complete. The full game follows Sarah's investigation into Helios Pharmaceuticals across multiple locations. The truth is larger than one hospital."*

## THE UNIQUE MECHANIC: VHS TAPE STORYTELLING

**How it works:**
1. CRT TVs are scattered throughout the hospital — break rooms, offices, nurse stations
2. VHS tapes are found in drawers, lockers, and hidden rooms
3. When a tape is inserted into a TV, the full CRT/VCR/VHS Effects system activates: scan lines, tracking distortion, color bleeding, static bursts
4. Each tape delivers a critical story beat through "found footage" — security cameras, corporate videos, clinical sessions
5. The tapes are numbered but found out of order, creating a fragmented narrative the player pieces together
6. Some tapes have hidden details only visible if the player pauses/rewinds (an interaction mechanic using the CRT system)
7. The final tape reveals the player character's identity

**Why this is special:**
- Analog horror (VHS aesthetics) is a massively popular genre right now (Mandela Catalogue, Backrooms, Local 58)
- The CRT TV asset was designed for visual effects — we're turning it into the core narrative device
- It solves the "no voice acting budget" problem: tapes can use text overlays and visual-only storytelling
- Each tape is a self-contained scene that can be developed and polished independently

---

## THE UNIQUE MECHANIC: PHONE AS GUIDE

**How it works:**
1. The Advanced Phone System V3 provides a realistic in-game phone interface
2. The anonymous caller sends texts that guide the player and deliver story
3. Early texts are helpful: "Go to the third floor"
4. Mid-game texts become unsettling: "You used to cry when you smelled antiseptic"
5. Late-game texts become desperate: "You need to REMEMBER"
6. The player can choose NOT to follow the phone's guidance — exploring on their own — but misses context
7. The phone also receives "error" messages and garbled texts that hint at the caller's emotional state

## WHY THIS WORKS FOR A SOLO DEV

1. **The Abandoned Hospital pack (46+ assets) is the largest single asset** in the library — it provides an entire game's worth of environments from one download
2. **Horror games thrive on darkness and limitation** — limited flashlight battery, narrow corridors, and darkness HIDE asset reuse and boundaries
3. **Zero combat development** — the "enemies" are ambiguous hallucinations using Zombie Lite, requiring no combat system tuning
4. **VHS tapes can be created with simple 2D overlays** on the CRT TV system — no 3D cinematics needed
5. **Phone text messages are the cheapest narrative delivery** — just UI text, but the Phone System makes it feel immersive
6. **Linear progression** means no branching level design, no open-world problems
7. **Horror is the most forgiving genre for indie devs** — players expect rough edges, low fidelity, and weirdness. It adds to the atmosphere.
8. **Analog horror is trending** — the VHS aesthetic alone generates interest on social media and YouTube
